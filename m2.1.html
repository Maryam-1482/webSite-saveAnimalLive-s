Panda

giant panda is a black and white bear living in China, a huge spherical, heavy-shouldered,
body-hoarding animal with a short tail, the panda has black fur on his ears,
around his eyes, snout, legs and arms, and the giantpanda lives in bamboo forests,
wet and sedema mountain cones at an altitude of 1,500-3,000 meters in central western and southern China.

Social behaviour

Giant pandas are isolated and gentle animals and rarely threaten anyone,
and pandas mix with each other only in the mating season, but recent studies have shown
that pandas may form groups of 7-15 and occupy an area.

Reproduction

The giant panda is looking for a partner in the spring to mate with her,
and in the fall the female panda gives birth to one puppy.
It is rare for pandas to give birth to twins except in a few cases,
and the puppy stays with his mother for a year and a half, sometimes longer.

Sichuan Giant Panda Shelters

Sichuan Giant Panda Sanctuaries are a UNESCO-sponsored World Heritage Site in the Kyunglai
and Jiajing Mountains, with a natural panda colony and several picturesque parks,
with more than 30% of the world's pandas living in these shelters.
